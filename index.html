The most inspiring friendship movie

The friendship movie that inspired me the most was YAARANA , directed by a famous director Rakesh Kumar. This film was the cynosure of all eyes during the early 80s. I consider this movie as one of the best examples of true friendship and camaraderie. It is an inspiration because it showed us that how a man prioritized his childhood friend over wealth and sold all his property. If we talk about the plot of this movie, the film was based on childhood friendship. A village simpleton man, whose city bred bestie helps him to become a renowned singer.

The first half of the movie could really tickle your funny bones, when Amitabh bachchan first time visits the city. The music album of this film was the cherry on top. Each and every song of this movie gives you a rejuvenating experience.

In the second half we could see that after selling all his assets and house for the sake of his friend, Amjad khan had turned insane. But Amitabh bachchan stood by his side and aided him well until he gained sanity. As the saying goes ‘A friend in need is a friend in deed’ this movie proved it right. This film has the strong ability to tug at your heart strings. The heart touching friendship showed in this movie is truly an inspiration for all the new generations. I strongly recommend all of you to watch this film atleast once.

The Epitome of an Ideal Teacher

If you're expecting this to be another student ranting about her teachers, then you're right. But let’s take a moment to imagine something different. What if our schools were filled with our idea of utopian teachers? I hadn’t considered it either, but think about it—a teacher who strolls into the classroom without any books because books are “boring.” They’re the ones who crack jokes, give easy grades, and seem more like your best buddy than an authority figure. With such relaxed attitudes, classrooms would feel less like school and more like a hangout spot. It sounds fun, doesn’t it?

But here’s where things get tricky. While having a super chill, easygoing teacher might seem like a dream come true, it can sometimes backfire. When a teacher becomes too relaxed, important lessons might be overlooked, discipline can slip, and students may end up feeling lost rather than guided. After all, hangout spots won’t enlighten us.

This is why the balance between being approachable and maintaining a structured learning environment is crucial. Our conventional, by-the-book teachers—those who stress over completing every part of the syllabus, even if it means going the extra mile to finish a Shakespearean sonnet—are doing so because they understand something important. They know that, for most of us, a sheet of paper may be the one to decide our future. They know when to be friendly but also when to enforce the rules, making them the ones we truly need. These teachers understand that education is about more than just having a good time; it’s about preparing us for what lies ahead.
And that’s what makes an ideal teacher.

Paridhi
